Model and derivation

This simulator is a deliberately simple hybrid dynamical model of the tabletop tipping-container demonstration. It is not a full rigid-body/fluid simulation. The goal is to capture the three ingredients that matter for the public demonstration: continuous drive, dissipation, and a nonlinear threshold/reset event.

1. State variables

The simulated state is the water volume V(t) in the container and a display angle θ(t). The angle is used for visualization and for the phase portrait. The water volume is the main physical state.

2. Geometry and water height

The container is modeled as a rectangular volume with length L, width W, and height H. The maximum volume is

V_max = L W H.

While the container is upright, the water height is approximated as

h(V) = V / (L W), clipped between 0 and H.

This ignores the changing free-surface shape when the bucket is tilted. That is acceptable for a first-order design model because the slow part of the cycle happens while the container is upright; the tilted part is treated as a fast dumping/reset event.

3. Inflow and leakage

The drive is a constant inflow Q_in. In optical-cavity language this is the analogue of the continuous laser drive. The dissipation is represented by leakage through an effective circular hole of diameter d_leak.

The total leak area is

A = π d_leak² / 4.

If the hole is at height h_leak above the bottom, only the water column above that hole contributes to the hydrostatic head. Using Torricelli's law with a discharge coefficient C_d ≈ 0.62, the leak flow is

Q_leak(h) = C_d A √(2 g max(h - h_leak, 0)).

Thus, while the container is upright and not tipping, the water-volume dynamics are

dV/dt = Q_in - Q_leak(h(V)).

This equation already has the first important behavior: if the inflow is low enough, leakage balances inflow at a steady water level and no tipping occurs.

4. Fixed point condition

A fixed point of the upright dynamics satisfies

Q_in = Q_leak(h*).

Solving the Torricelli expression gives the approximate fixed-point height

h* = h_leak + (Q_in / (C_d A))² / (2 g),

provided the leak area is nonzero and h* lies within the container. If this fixed point lies below the tipping height, the demonstration sits in the fixed-point regime: water flows in continuously, but the level approaches a constant value.

5. Torque balance and tipping threshold

The tipping event is modeled using a simple torque balance about the pivot. Let d_w be the effective horizontal lever arm of the water center of mass, m_cw the counterweight mass, d_cw its lever arm, m_c the empty container mass, and d_c the empty-container restoring lever arm.

The destabilizing moment from the water is approximated as

M_water = ρ V d_w.

The restoring moment is approximated as

M_restore = m_cw d_cw + m_c d_c.

The gravitational acceleration cancels because both sides are gravitational torques. The tipping threshold is therefore estimated by M_water = M_restore, giving

V_tip = (m_cw d_cw + m_c d_c) / (ρ d_w).

This is the main design formula. Increasing the counterweight mass or counterweight arm raises the tipping volume. Increasing the water lever arm lowers the tipping volume. In a real setup, the water center of mass moves with fill level and tilt angle; here it is represented by one effective lever arm so the threshold remains easy to tune and interpret.

6. Hybrid switching: upright, tipped, reset

The system is hybrid: it follows one continuous differential equation while upright, then switches state when the tipping threshold is crossed.

upright → tipped when V ≥ V_tip and V_tip ≤ V_max.

Once tipped, the model assumes the container dumps quickly. The simulator uses an approximate tipped-state volume equation of the form

dV/dt = Q_eff - Q_dump - 0.35 Q_leak.

Here Q_dump is controlled by the dump-rate slider. The factor 0.35 simply says that the small-hole leakage is less important during the fast dumping event. The inflow is reduced to an effective fraction because, when the bucket is tipped, some incoming water misses the container or enters at a geometry-dependent location.

The reset rule is

tipped → upright when V ≤ V_reset, with V_reset = 0.18 V_tip.

The gap between V_tip and V_reset is the hysteresis that makes the oscillation clean. Without this hysteresis the bucket would chatter near the threshold instead of producing a robust cycle.

7. Pinned or overdriven behavior

At very high inflow, the real apparatus may fail to reset: the incoming stream can keep the container in the tipped configuration, or it can refill it while it is still dumping. The simulator represents this with a simple empirical pinned-state condition:

pinned if pin_factor × Q_in / Q_dump > 0.85 and V > 0.8 V_reset.

This is not a fundamental law. It is a tunable way to display the practical overdriven regime that is likely to occur in the physical demonstrator.

8. Regime diagram in one parameter

A useful dimensionless drive parameter is

D = Q_in / Q_leak(h_tip).

where h_tip = V_tip / (L W). The simulator reports this value in the status panel.

D < 1Stable water level below thresholdLoss balances drive before the nonlinear tipping event
D ≳ 1 and reset possibleRepeated fill, tip, dump, resetStable relaxation oscillation / limit cycle analogue
Very large Q_in / Q_dumpPinned or irregular tipped stateOverdriven apparatus; reset no longer clean

9. Period estimate

In the clean limit-cycle regime, most of the period is the slow filling time. The exact estimate is

T_fill = ∫ from V_reset to V_tip dV / (Q_in - Q_leak(V)).

If leakage varies slowly, a useful napkin approximation is

T ≈ (V_tip - V_reset) / (Q_in - Q_leak,avg) + T_dump.

This explains why increasing inflow shortens the oscillation period, while increasing leak rate lengthens the period and eventually destroys the oscillation by creating a fixed point below threshold.

10. Angle and phase portrait

The plotted angle is a display variable rather than a full rotational equation of motion. The simulator moves the angle smoothly toward an upright value or a tipped value after the hybrid switch. The phase plot θ versus V is therefore best interpreted as a visualization of the cycle: slow filling along the upright branch, rapid transition to the tipped branch, dumping, and reset.

11. Important limitations

  • The water center of mass is represented by a single effective lever arm.
  • Sloshing, impact, friction, pivot bearing losses, and changing free-surface shape are ignored.
  • The pinned-state model is empirical and only meant to show a plausible high-drive failure regime.
  • The schematic drawing is illustrative; the slider values drive the equations, not the exact on-screen geometry.

Despite these simplifications, the model captures the intended physics message: a continuously driven dissipative nonlinear system can settle to a fixed point, develop a stable repeating cycle, or enter an overdriven regime as the drive and loss parameters are varied.
