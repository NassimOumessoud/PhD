Tipping-bucket rain gauge limit-cycle simulator

A two-bucket relaxation oscillator. A fixed nozzle continuously feeds the lower bucket; both buckets leak through small holes; when the lower bucket reaches a threshold the seesaw flips, that bucket dumps, and the other bucket becomes active.

Model and derivation

Internal derived quantities: the tipping volume is now obtained from a torque balance that includes the single-bucket bare mass slider. Retained volume after spill, dump outflow, and overdrive condition are derived inside the script. The only remaining mechanical control is the restoring/switching time, which sets how quickly the insert moves between the two stable tilted states.

This is a hybrid relaxation-oscillator model of a tipping-bucket rain gauge with dissipation. It is intentionally simpler and more robust than the single counterweighted bucket: the two stable mechanical states are built into the seesaw geometry.

1. State variables

The reported bucket volume follows the bucket currently receiving Q_in. During a flip, this is the receiving bucket, not the bucket that is simultaneously dumping.

The state is the pair of bucket volumes V_L(t), V_R(t), and the seesaw angle theta(t). Positive angle means the left bucket is lower; negative angle means the right bucket is lower.

Q_incontinuous flow from the fixed nozzle

2. Geometry and threshold

Each bucket is approximated as a rectangular trough with length L, width W, and height H.

V_max = L W H

The tipping volume is set as a fraction of capacity:

V_tip = f_tip V_max

This replaces a full torque calculation. In a real rain gauge, V_tip is set by bucket shape, pivot placement, friction, stops, and center of mass.

3. Leakage

For an effective circular leak hole of diameter d, area A = pi d^2 / 4, and height h_leak, Torricelli flow gives

Q_leak(V) = C_d A sqrt(2 g max(V/(LW) - h_leak, 0))

Both buckets leak at all times.

4. Filling phase

Let a be the lower active bucket and b the other bucket. During filling,

dV_a/dt = Q_in - Q_leak(V_a)
dV_b/dt = - Q_leak(V_b)

If the active bucket reaches a fixed point below threshold, the system does not tip.

Q_in = Q_leak(V_*), with V_* < V_tip

5. Switching and dumping

When V_a >= V_tip, the target angle changes sign. The filled bucket is lifted and dumps toward a residual volume, while the other bucket becomes the receiver:

dV_dump/dt = -(V_dump - V_reset)/tau_dump - Q_leak(V_dump)
dV_receive/dt = Q_in - Q_leak(V_receive)

The angle relaxes toward the opposite stable side with a finite switching time.

6. Regimes

A convenient drive parameter is

D = Q_in / Q_leak(V_tip)
